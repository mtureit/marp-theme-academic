## Slide 4 — Paper Reading Findings
*(~90 seconds)*

Now, let me explain what we learned from the two papers.

The first paper by Keysar et al. asks this question:
Does using a foreign language reduce decision biases?
Their answer was yes.
When people use a foreign language, a bias called the "framing effect" and another called "loss aversion" become smaller.
This means people make more logical and less emotional decisions in a foreign language.

The second paper by Costa et al. asks a similar question:
Does language affect moral judgment?
They used a famous thought experiment called the "footbridge dilemma."
In this experiment, a person must choose whether to sacrifice one person to save five others.
Their finding was that people made more logical choices when they used a foreign language.
Again, this is because a foreign language reduces emotional feelings.

So, what does this mean for our research?
Both papers show that the Foreign Language Effect is real — in human participants.
Our research asks the next question:
Does the same effect appear when an LLM *simulates* a human's decisions?
The tasks used in these papers are also good candidates for our AI interview design.

## Slide 5 — AI Interview Design & Questions for Prof. Tho
*(~70 seconds)*

Next, let me talk about our AI interview design.

We have decided to avoid topics that are politically or historically sensitive.
This is important for cross-cultural research between Japan and Vietnam.
We want to choose topics that feel comfortable and natural for students in both countries.

Right now, our candidate theme is "multicultural co-learning" — this means learning together with students from different cultures.
We think this theme is familiar for university students in both Japan and Vietnam.
It is also a neutral topic that does not touch on political or historical issues.

However, we are not fully sure about this choice.
So I have two questions for you, Professor Tho.

First, is multicultural co-learning a suitable and natural theme for Vietnamese students?

Second, what decision scenarios would you recommend around this theme?
