## Paper Reading Findings

### Summary of the 2 papers

| Paper | Research Question | Key Finding |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Foreign Language Effect** Keysar et al. (2012), *Psych. Science* | Does using a foreign language reduce decision biases? | FLE confirmed: framing effect and loss aversion **significantly reduced** in a foreign language; more analytic, less emotion-driven decisions |
| **Your Morals Depend on Language** Costa et al. (2014), *PLOS ONE* | Does language (L1 vs. L2) affect moral judgment? | More **utilitarian choices** in a foreign language in the footbridge dilemma; attributed to reduced emotional response |

<div class="bubble">

**Relevance to our research:**  These two papers establish FLE in *human* participants. Our research asks the next question — <span class="marker">does the same effect appear when LLMs *simulate* human decision-making?</span> The decision and moral dilemma tasks used here are also useful references for our AI interview design.

</div>
